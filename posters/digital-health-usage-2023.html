Digital health usage and information-sharing behaviors: A national survey data analysis

Tolentino, D. A., & Jiang, Y. (2023). Published in Communicating Nursing Research, 56.

Digital healthHealth informationNational survey
The big idea

Who uses digital health tools, and who shares their health data, and with whom? A national survey takes a look.

Who
3,865 U.S. adults (national survey)
How
Cross-sectional analysis of HINTS data
What we learned
Factors linked to digital health use and data sharing

In plain language

Digital health tools can improve health, but adoption is uneven and people have real concerns about privacy and access. We analyzed a national survey of 3,865 U.S. adults to learn which personal, health, and technology factors are linked to using digital health tools and to sharing health information with providers, with family and friends, or on social media.

About this study

We used data from the Health Information National Trends Survey (HINTS 5, Cycle 4; N = 3,865), collected February to June 2020, and applied weighted logistic regression to identify factors associated with digital health usage and information-sharing behaviors. Nearly three-quarters of respondents reported using a digital health tool.

Key themes

1

Uneven adoption

Digital health use varies across groups.

2

Sharing with whom

People share data differently with providers, family, and social media.

3

Equity and trust

Privacy, access, and experience shape use.
