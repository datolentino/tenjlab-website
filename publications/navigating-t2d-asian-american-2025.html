Navigating type 2 diabetes care: Asian American perspectives on self-management education and support

Tolentino, D. A., Boy, P., Long, S., Roca, R. P. E., III, Peña, M., Sidhu, M., Luzuriaga, M., Palao, G. M., Palao, G. J., & Choi, S. (2025).

DiabetesAsian American healthQualitative study
Read the full article
The big idea

Diabetes education works best when it is built around the real facilitators and barriers Asian Americans face, including cost and culturally relevant support.

Who
Asian Americans with type 2 diabetes
How
Qualitative interviews
What we learned
What helps and what gets in the way of using diabetes education

In plain language

We asked Asian Americans with type 2 diabetes what helps and what gets in the way when they try to use diabetes education and support programs, so those programs can serve them better.

About this study

Asian Americans face a disproportionate burden of type 2 diabetes, yet very few people take part in formal diabetes self-management education and support (DSMES). We interviewed Asian American patients about what shapes whether they engage with these programs.

Participants described barriers such as cost, stigma, and a lack of culturally tailored resources, alongside the supports that help them stay engaged. The findings point toward more accessible, culturally responsive programs.

In the news: Physician’s Weekly featured this study.

Key themes

1

What helps

Trusted, culturally relevant education and support that fits patients’ lives.

2

What gets in the way

Cost, stigma, and a lack of resources tailored to Asian American experiences.

3

Many levels

Influences span individual, family, and community contexts.
