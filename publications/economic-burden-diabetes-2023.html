Publication · Preventive Medicine Reports · 2023

Economic burden, financial stress, and cost-related coping among people with uncontrolled diabetes

Patel, M. R., Tolentino, D. A., Smith, A., & Heisler, M. (2023).

DiabetesFinancial hardshipSurvey
Read the full article
The big idea

Hard-to-control diabetes comes with real financial strain, and many people turn to cost-related coping, such as cutting back on care.

Who
U.S. adults with uncontrolled (high A1c) diabetes
How
Baseline survey data, 2019 to 2021
What we learned
The financial toll of diabetes and how people cope

In plain language

This study examined the financial strain of living with hard-to-control diabetes, and the difficult tradeoffs people make, such as skipping medication, to cope with the cost.

About this study

This study took a close look at economic burden, financial stress, and cost-related coping among people living with high A1c (uncontrolled) diabetes.

Detailed information about financial and material hardship can help shape policies, clinical practice, and interventions that better support people in managing their diabetes.

Key themes

1

Economic burden

The everyday cost of managing diabetes adds up.

2

Financial stress

Money worries weigh heavily on people with diabetes.

3

Cost-related coping

Some cut back on care or medication to manage costs.
