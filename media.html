In the media

Features, interviews, and public scholarship that share the lab’s work on diabetes, community health, and nursing informatics with wider audiences.

Features, mentions, and appearances

  • Feature Physician’s Weekly June 10, 2025

    Reimagining type 2 diabetes self-management to tackle cost and access barriers

    Dr. Tolentino makes the case that diabetes self-management education and support, one of the most effective tools for controlling type 2 diabetes, too often stays out of reach for Asian American communities because of cost and limited access. He calls for rethinking how these programs are designed and delivered so more people can actually use them.

    Read the article →
  • Featured expert Daily Bruin April 22, 2025

    Beyond the statute: why health care needs systemic reform to promote equity

    In this Daily Bruin story on equity in health care, Dr. Tolentino explains why closing health disparities takes more than new policies. He argues for race-conscious care that names racism as a health factor and shifts decision-making power toward the communities most affected.

    Read the article →
  • Feature The Global Filipino Magazine March 21, 2025

    Nurse leader advocates for integrating informatics into healthcare

    This profile highlights Dr. Tolentino’s call to weave informatics and digital health skills throughout nursing education and practice. He encourages nurses to explore careers beyond the bedside and to use technology to close gaps in care.

    Read the article →
  • Podcast Clinical Appraisal Podcast 2021

    Nursing informatics and health equity

    Dr. Tolentino joins the Clinical Appraisal Podcast to talk about his path into nursing informatics as a nurse informaticist, and how data and digital tools can be used to advance health equity.

Popular writing

  • Magazine article Positively Filipino January 22, 2025

    Managing diabetes among Filipinos in the diaspora

    By Marjorie Luzuriaga and Dante A. Tolentino

    Written for the Filipino diaspora, this article offers practical, culturally grounded tips for living well with diabetes, like adapting beloved Filipino dishes with healthier swaps such as cauliflower rice. The takeaway: steady self-management, regular movement, and a strong partnership with your care team make a full, healthy life possible.
